I am an assistant professor at the Department of Computer Science, NYU Shanghai (Starting at Fall 2023). I am also affiliated at Center for Data Science, New York University.
I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Computer Science, New York University, mentored by Prof. Kyunghyun Cho and Prof. He He. I am a member of the ML2 lab and the CILVR group.
I received my Ph.D. at the Department of Computer Science, University of Maryland, College Park, advised by Prof. Jordan Boyd-Graber and Prof. Hal Daumé III. Throughout my graduate studies, I spent several summers doing internships in industry, including Microsoft Semantic Machines, DMX group and IDEAS group at Microsoft Research.
My main research interest is in natural language processing. My goal is to equip language model based intelligent agents with external tools to make them more reliable and interpretable in real scenarios. Some directions I'm excited recently include:

(1) Agentic Search and RL: Real-world tasks often require agents to perform multiple, iterative searches across diverse sources. How to effectively retrieve and incorporate external knowledge, especially when the task demands intensive reasoning capabilities?

(2) Diffusion Language Models: Diffusion language models show strong potential in both reasoning and efficiency, but how can they be integrated into agentic scenarios?

(3) Understanding Language Models in knowledge and reasoning: How language models internally acquire knowledge, reason about it, and adopt quickly with new information (i.e. Knowledge Editing)?

(4) Human-centered NLP in Healthcare: How to have both language model and human in-the-loop to reliably solve real-world healthcare problems (e.g., Radiology Report Generation, Medical Agents)?
